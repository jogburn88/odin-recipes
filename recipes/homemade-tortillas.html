Homemade Tortillas

Here's to making your own tortillas

One of the items that makes latino food special is a good homemade tortilla. They are way better than the ones that you find in the supermarket. Homemade tortillas are generally a little thicker and a bit less dense. The ingredients are simple and process only takes a few minutes.

Here is a recipe that I use that I really like. They use lard which I think adds a better texture and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp lard
  • 1 1/2 cups water

Steps

  1. Wisk together the flour, salt and baking powder in a large mixing bowl
  2. Add the lard and mix into the flour with your finger until the flour is completely coated and feels smooth in your fingers
  3. Add the water and mix with a fork until everything comes together into a smooth ball of dough. Turn it onto a floured surface and knead for a minute or two until it starts to feel smooth.
  4. Divide the dough into 24 pieces or fewer for the larger tortillas and then roll into balls and cover with plastic wrap. Let rest for around 10 minutes.
  5. Roll each dough ball until it is ver thin around 1/16" thick
  6. Place on a dry very hot skillet (around 550F) just until bubbles start to form and little dark brown spots start to form. Flip it over and let the other side start to get the brown spots then remove from the skillet.
  7. Place in something like a tortilla warmer of between two pieces of tinfoil to keep them warm until you are ready to serve.
